# 1MEAL Website Brief / Handoff

## Purpose of this document
This document consolidates the important information we have discussed so far about **1MEAL** so the website can reflect the idea as accurately as possible.

It is intentionally limited to points we have actually discussed. Where something is not yet fully fixed, it is marked clearly so the website can stay strong without inventing details.

---

## 1. Core idea
**1MEAL** is a food startup concept centered around a **meal-in-a-bar** product.

The core promise we discussed repeatedly is:
- **one bar = one full meal**
- a **convenient, on-the-go solution**
- something that feels more like a **real meal** than a typical snack bar or replacement drink

The project is meant to respond to situations where people do **not have the time to eat properly** and therefore either:
- skip a meal,
- snack badly,
- or use a drink-based replacement that does not feel satisfying in the same way as eating an actual meal.

---

## 2. Problem 1MEAL is trying to solve
The recurring problem discussed is very clear:

Many people live with **busy, packed schedules** and regularly fail to eat properly.

Examples we discussed:
- students running between classes, study sessions, deadlines, or other commitments
- corporate / working people running from meeting to meeting
- people with intense daily schedules who want something practical but still nutritionally serious

The underlying pain point is not only lack of time. It is also the frustration that the alternatives often feel unsatisfactory:
- skipping meals can hurt energy and focus
- random snacking often leads to poor food choices
- meal replacement drinks may be convenient, but they do not feel like a real meal experience

A key angle from our discussions is that **1MEAL should feel more substantial and more "real" than liquid meal replacements**.

---

## 3. Product concept
The product concept we discussed is:
- a **bar format**
- positioned not as a simple snack, but as a **complete meal solution**
- meant to be **practical, durable, portable, and easy to consume on the go**

Important nuance:
- it should not come across as just another protein bar
- it should not come across as a candy-like energy bar
- it should not come across as a purely gym niche product
- it should feel like a **modern, premium, functional food product**

There is a strong emphasis on the fact that the product should combine:
- convenience
- satiety
- nutritional seriousness
- a real-food / meal-like perception

---

## 4. Positioning
The positioning we have discussed points toward:

### Primary positioning
A **premium, convenient, nutritionally serious meal bar** for people who do not have the time or conditions to eat properly.

### What it is *not*
The brand should avoid being framed as:
- cheap mass-market convenience food
- only a sports nutrition product
- only a protein bar
- a gimmicky startup snack
- a purely technical "fuel" object with no appetite appeal

### What it should feel like
The ideal perception is closer to:
- elevated convenience
- modern wellness
- ambitious lifestyle
- efficient but desirable nutrition
- premium everyday utility

## 14. Brand naming / architecture notes
We discussed a very important naming consideration for the brand.

### Important trademark reality we discussed
Descriptive names like:
- 1Meal
- 1Meal-Bar
- Meal Bar
- Full Meal Bar

are likely **weak from a trademark/protectability perspective**.

### Direction we discussed instead
Use a **distinctive master brand** and place the descriptive product wording underneath.

Examples we discussed include:
- **AMO**
- **OneFuel**
- **UrFuel**
- **Velora**

Of these, we discussed **AMO** as especially interesting because:
- it is more distinctive,
- it feels premium/minimal,
- and it can sit above a descriptive product line.

### Example brand architecture we discussed
- **AMO**  
  *1Meal-Bar*  
  or  
  *One Bar = One Full Meal*

### Website implication
The website should ideally be built in a way that allows:
- a strong master brand,
- and a descriptive subline underneath,
- without locking everything into a legally weak descriptive product name.

This is strategically important.
